# Week 6 – Job Stories

## S1 – Overwhelmed by deadlines
**When** I have multiple coursework deadlines in the same week  
**I want to** see which tasks are most urgent and important  
**So that** I can focus without feeling overwhelmed.

- Evidence: P1 notes (lines …)
- Inclusion risk: Cognitive, Mental health (anxiety)

## S2 – Working on the move / without a mouse
**When** I’m on a laptop without a mouse or I’m using the keyboard only  
**I want to** manage tasks without needing precise pointer clicks  
**So that** I can still keep on top of work when I’m travelling or tired.

- Evidence: P2 notes (lines …)
- Inclusion risk: Keyboard, Motor

## S3 – Remembering what’s important
**When** I haven’t looked at my task list for a few days  
**I want to** be reminded of urgent or overdue tasks in a calm way  
**So that** I don’t miss critical deadlines or feel blindsided.

- Evidence: P1 notes (lines …)
- Inclusion risk: Cognitive, Mental health

## S4 – Low-friction “quick add”
**When** I’m in a lecture or busy context  
**I want to** quickly capture a short task with minimal fields  
**So that** I don’t forget it later.

- Evidence: P2 notes (lines …)
- Inclusion risk: Cognitive

## S5 – Clear feedback that actions worked
**When** I add or delete a task  
**I want to** see a clear confirmation message that doesn’t disappear too fast  
**So that** I know what happened and don’t have to double-check.

- Evidence: P1 notes (lines …)
- Inclusion risk: Screen reader (SR), Cognitive

## S6 – Accessible visual design (optional extra)
**When** I’m tired or have difficulty reading dense text  
**I want to** see tasks with good spacing and contrast  
**So that** I can scan my list without eye strain.

- Evidence: P3 notes (lines …)
- Inclusion risk: Low vision, Cognitive
